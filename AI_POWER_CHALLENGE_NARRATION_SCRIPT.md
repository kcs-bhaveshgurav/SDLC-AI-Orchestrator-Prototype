# AI Power Challenge Narration Script

**Product:** SDLC AI Orchestrator  
**Tagline:** Transform Requirements into Delivery Artifacts with AI  
**Target duration:** 5 to 7 minutes  
**Recording style:** Confident enterprise product demo for CTOs, CIOs, enterprise customers, and judges.

---

## 0:00-0:25 - Opening

**Screen name:** Opening / Product Introduction  
**What is visible:** Start on the product entry or first visible platform screen.  
**Action:** Pause briefly, then begin the narration before navigating into the platform.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"In enterprise software delivery, one of the biggest challenges is turning raw client requirements into clear, consistent, delivery-ready artifacts. Business analysts create BRDs, product teams create PRDs, developers need technical prompts, QA teams need test cases, and leadership needs traceability and governance. Today, much of that work is still manual, fragmented, and difficult to audit.

SDLC AI Orchestrator solves this by using specialized AI agents to transform requirements into complete delivery artifacts, while preserving traceability, governance, change impact, and enterprise control.

Let's see how the platform works."

**Why this feature is important:** Sets the business problem and product vision.  
**Business value:** Positions the prototype as an enterprise AI delivery platform, not just a document generator.  
**Transition:** "I'll start by signing in as a Platform Admin."

---

## 0:25-0:40 - Login

**Screen name:** Login  
**What is visible:** Enterprise branding, secure login options, SSO, and role-based access messaging.  
**Action:** Select Platform Admin login or simulate login into the main platform.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"Here I start with secure authentication. In an enterprise environment, access control matters because different users need different capabilities. A platform administrator can manage providers, teams, budgets, governance, and audit controls, while project users focus on requirements and delivery artifacts.

For this demo, I'll log in as a Platform Admin."

**Why this feature is important:** Shows enterprise readiness and role separation.  
**Business value:** Supports secure adoption across regulated organizations.  
**Transition:** "Now we land in the AI Control Tower."

---

## 0:40-1:10 - Platform Admin Dashboard

**Screen name:** Platform Admin Dashboard / AI Control Tower  
**What is visible:** Platform statistics, organizations, teams, projects, active users, enterprise workload distribution, AI usage, cost trend, provider health, and activity feed.  
**Action:** Show the dashboard KPIs and briefly point to the Enterprise workload distribution panel.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"Here you can see the AI Control Tower for the entire enterprise. This gives administrators one place to monitor organizations, teams, projects, active users, AI usage, cost trends, and provider health.

The Enterprise workload distribution view helps leaders understand where AI delivery activity is happening across tenants and teams. On the right, the activity stream shows operational events such as provider updates, budget alerts, access changes, and prompt approvals.

This allows platform administrators to manage the complete enterprise from one place instead of jumping across separate tools."

**Why this feature is important:** Centralizes enterprise AI operations.  
**Business value:** Improves governance, visibility, and control across multiple projects.  
**Transition:** "Next, I'll open AI Provider Management."

---

## 1:10-1:35 - AI Provider Management

**Screen name:** AI Provider Management  
**What is visible:** OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, Azure OpenAI, model status, default models, fallback models, uptime, spend, token limits, and budget indicators.  
**Action:** Open the AI Providers page and point to provider cards and settings.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"This is where the enterprise manages AI providers such as OpenAI, Claude, Gemini, and Azure OpenAI. Administrators can define default models, fallback models, token limits, budget limits, and provider health rules.

This is important because enterprise AI cannot depend on one model for every task. Requirement analysis, architecture reasoning, test generation, and executive summaries may each need different routing rules.

With this control layer, organizations can balance quality, cost, security, and performance."

**Why this feature is important:** Shows enterprise-grade AI control.  
**Business value:** Reduces cost risk and improves model reliability.  
**Transition:** "Now let's move from platform configuration to team management."

---

## 1:35-1:55 - Team Management

**Screen name:** Team Management  
**What is visible:** Business Analysis, Development, QA, DevOps, and Architecture teams with managers, budgets, projects, and performance indicators.  
**Action:** Open Team Management and select or point to one team card.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"Here you can see team-level management. Each team has an assigned manager, project load, budget allocation, and performance metrics.

For example, the Business Analysis team may focus on BRDs, PRDs, and requirement quality, while the QA team focuses on test generation, coverage, and regression confidence.

This gives leadership a clear way to allocate AI resources and budget by team."

**Why this feature is important:** Connects AI operations to real enterprise teams.  
**Business value:** Helps managers plan capacity, budget, and accountability.  
**Transition:** "Next, I'll open the Project Portfolio Center."

---

## 1:55-2:15 - Project Portfolio

**Screen name:** Project Portfolio Center  
**What is visible:** Multiple projects, project health, risks, flow scores, views for grid, timeline, roadmap, and dependencies.  
**Action:** Open Portfolio Center and select or highlight one project, such as Omni-Claims.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"This is the Project Portfolio Center. Here, program leaders can see multiple projects in one view, compare project health, monitor risk, track progress, and understand delivery confidence.

I can switch between grid, timeline, roadmap, and dependency views depending on whether I am managing execution, planning releases, or looking for cross-project risk.

For this walkthrough, I'll focus on one project and move into its workspace."

**Why this feature is important:** Gives portfolio-level visibility.  
**Business value:** Helps executives prioritize investment and reduce delivery risk.  
**Transition:** "Let's open the Project Workspace."

---

## 2:15-2:35 - Project Workspace

**Screen name:** Project Workspace  
**What is visible:** Requirements, BRDs, PRDs, user stories, architecture, developer prompts, test cases, traceability, impact analysis, approvals, and reports.  
**Action:** Open Project Workspace and point across the artifact tiles.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"Every project has its own isolated workspace. Here you can see the full SDLC structure: requirements, BRDs, PRDs, user stories, architecture, developer prompts, test cases, traceability, impact analysis, approvals, and reports.

The key point is that all downstream artifacts are connected back to the original requirement, so teams are not managing disconnected documents."

**Why this feature is important:** Establishes the project as a governed delivery container.  
**Business value:** Reduces fragmentation and keeps delivery teams aligned.  
**Transition:** "Now I'll demonstrate how a requirement enters the system."

---

## 2:35-3:10 - Requirement Intelligence Studio

**Screen name:** Requirement Intelligence Studio  
**What is visible:** Upload area, supported file types, quality score, ambiguity detection, missing alerts, duplicate detection, requirement graph, and AI recommendations.  
**Action:** Open Requirement Studio and simulate uploading a sample requirement.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"This is the Requirement Intelligence Studio. I can upload raw client requirements from PDFs, documents, spreadsheets, meeting notes, or existing backlog items.

Once uploaded, the platform analyzes quality, detects ambiguity, identifies missing non-functional requirements, finds duplicate requirement clusters, and builds an initial requirement knowledge graph.

Here you can see recommendations such as splitting a requirement into clearer atomic rules, attaching the right policy source, and triggering BRD and PRD regeneration after confirmation."

**Why this feature is important:** Converts unstructured input into governed requirements.  
**Business value:** Saves analyst time and improves requirement quality before delivery begins.  
**Transition:** "After the requirement is analyzed, the AI orchestration workflow begins."

---

## 3:10-4:00 - AI Orchestration Center

**Screen name:** AI Orchestration Center  
**What is visible:** Nine-agent pipeline, agent status, models, cost, tokens, quality score, duration, and streaming response trace.  
**Action:** Open AI Orchestration and slowly show each agent in sequence.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"This is the hero workflow of the platform. The requirement now flows through a chain of specialized AI agents.

First, the Requirement Agent normalizes the input. Then the BRD Agent generates business requirements. The PRD Agent converts that into product features. The Story Agent creates user stories and acceptance criteria. The Architecture Agent prepares technical direction. The Developer Agent creates implementation prompts. The QA Agent generates test cases. The Security Agent validates risk and controls. Finally, the DevOps Agent prepares deployment guidance.

Each agent shows its model, cost, tokens, duration, and quality score, so the enterprise can monitor both output quality and AI spend."

**Why this feature is important:** Demonstrates the AI-native SDLC automation engine.  
**Business value:** Compresses weeks of manual documentation and coordination into a governed workflow.  
**Transition:** "Now let's look at the generated delivery artifacts."

---

## 4:00-4:25 - Generated Artifacts

**Screen name:** Generated Artifacts / Project Workspace Artifacts  
**What is visible:** BRDs, PRDs, user stories, developer prompts, QA test cases, architecture, approvals, and reports.  
**Action:** Return to Project Workspace or point to artifact sections across the project.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"The output of the orchestration workflow is a complete set of delivery-ready artifacts. From the original requirement, the platform generates BRDs, PRDs, user stories, developer prompts, QA test cases, architecture guidance, and reports.

Because these artifacts come from the same requirement graph, they stay consistent with each other. If the requirement changes later, the platform knows exactly which artifacts are affected."

**Why this feature is important:** Shows the system creates usable SDLC outputs, not just summaries.  
**Business value:** Reduces handoff errors between business, product, engineering, and QA.  
**Transition:** "To prove that consistency, let's open Traceability Galaxy."

---

## 4:25-5:00 - Traceability Galaxy

**Screen name:** Traceability Galaxy  
**What is visible:** Requirement-to-feature-to-story-to-prompt-to-test-case graph, zoom controls, pan, expand/collapse, impact path highlighting, and inspector panel.  
**Action:** Open Traceability Galaxy, select a requirement, and click Impact Path.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"Here you can see Traceability Galaxy. This view connects the original requirement to features, user stories, developer prompts, and test cases.

I'll select a requirement and highlight the impact path. Now we can see the complete chain of evidence from requirement to delivery artifact.

This is extremely important for regulated industries because teams can prove why a feature exists, which requirement it came from, and which tests validate it."

**Why this feature is important:** Makes traceability visual and interactive.  
**Business value:** Improves audit readiness, compliance, and delivery confidence.  
**Transition:** "Now I'll demonstrate what happens when a requirement changes."

---

## 5:00-5:35 - Change Impact Analysis

**Screen name:** Change Impact Simulator / Impact Analysis Engine  
**What is visible:** Requirement V1, Requirement V2, detected changes, affected BRDs, PRDs, stories, prompts, tests, risk indicators, heatmap, and regeneration recommendations.  
**Action:** Open Impact Simulator and show V1 versus V2 and the affected artifact heatmap.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"In real projects, requirements change constantly. Here the platform compares Requirement V1 with Requirement V2 and identifies what changed.

Instead of regenerating everything, it shows the affected BRD sections, PRD features, user stories, developer prompts, test cases, and security controls. The heatmap shows impact intensity, and the recommendation panel tells the team what should be regenerated.

This allows organizations to make precise changes instead of restarting the entire documentation process."

**Why this feature is important:** Controls downstream change impact.  
**Business value:** Saves time, reduces rework, and protects delivery schedules.  
**Transition:** "Next, let's look at how the platform uses enterprise knowledge."

---

## 5:35-5:55 - Knowledge Brain

**Screen name:** Knowledge Brain  
**What is visible:** Private SDLC memory graph, BRDs, PRDs, SOPs, architecture docs, coding standards, support docs, retrieval precision, citation density, and freshness.  
**Action:** Open Knowledge Brain and select one knowledge source.  

**What I should say while recording:**  
"This is the Knowledge Brain. It acts as the organization's private SDLC memory. The platform can retrieve previous BRDs, PRDs, SOPs, architecture documents, coding standards, and best practices.

This means generated artifacts are not generic. They are grounded in the organization's standards, prior delivery history, and approved documentation."

**Why this feature is important:** Grounds AI output in enterprise context.  
**Business value:** Improves accuracy, reuse, consistency, and governance.  
**Transition:** "Finally, let's move to the executive view."

---

## Recording Tips

- Keep the mouse movement slow and intentional.
- Pause for one second after each screen loads.
- Do not read every metric. Highlight only the strongest two or three data points.
- Use the phrase "This allows organizations to..." when moving from feature to value.
- Keep the AI Orchestration Center and Traceability Galaxy slightly longer than other sections because they are the strongest demo moments.
